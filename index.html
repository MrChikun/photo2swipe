Photo Deleter — Privacy Policy

Effective date: July 16, 2026

Photo Deleter is designed so that your photos and your data stay on your device. This policy explains what the app does and does not do.

Data we collect

None. Photo Deleter has no user accounts, no analytics, no tracking, and no advertising SDKs. The app makes no network requests and transmits nothing off your device.

Photo library access

Photo Deleter asks for access to your photo library solely so you can review your own photos and choose which to keep or delete. All browsing, sorting, and deletion happens locally on your device through Apple's Photos framework. Your photos are never uploaded, copied, or shared anywhere by this app.

Deletion

Photos you choose to delete are removed via the iOS system deletion dialog, which always asks for your confirmation. Deleted photos move to your device's own "Recently Deleted" album, where iOS retains them for about 30 days and where you can restore them at any time.

Data stored on your device

The app stores only small local preferences on your device: your language and appearance choices, your review progress, and lifetime statistics (such as how many photos you have deleted). This information never leaves your device and is removed when you delete the app.

Children's privacy

The app collects no data from anyone, including children.

Changes to this policy

If a future version of the app changes any of the above — for example, by introducing advertising — this policy will be updated before that version is released, and the app will ask for any newly required permissions.
